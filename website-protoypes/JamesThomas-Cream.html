Pathfinding Under Uncertainty: Why Robots Crash

Moving beyond A* geometric perfection to probabilistic survival.

In computer science curriculums, we are taught that A* (A-Star) is optimal. It finds the shortest path between two nodes in a graph. And in a digital vacuum—where motors never slip, sensors never drift, and wind doesn't exist—it is indeed perfect.

But when I tried to apply this logic to my latest robotics simulation, the results were catastrophic. The "optimal" path often skirted dangerous edges to save a few pixels of distance. In the real world, "optimal" is often synonymous with "risky."

To solve this, we must abandon the idea of a "Path" and embrace the "Policy." By using a Markov Decision Process (MDP), we stop telling the robot where to go and start telling it how to value its state.
